Reflection

📌 PRELIM REFLECTION

During the prelim period, I learned about Data Warehousing, the Design Process, Warehouse Architecture, Warehouse Models, Metadata, Schema Design, and OLAP. The technical topics were challenging at first, especially understanding schema design and how the architecture functions.

What made this term meaningful was that our activities required more than just memorization. We had to apply the concepts to real-world scenarios, which helped me understand their practical value.

📌 MIDTERM REFLECTION

During midterms, we worked on a Project-Based Test with my Capstone group. We were given four tasks to complete as a team.

This term felt easier for me because the lessons focused on basic data computations like SUM, COUNT, MIN, MAX, MEAN, MEDIAN, MODE, and ranking. These topics were straightforward and easy to grasp.

📌 FINAL TERM REFLECTION

For the final term, we were tasked to create an E-Portfolio using Google Sites. The portfolio includes our personal profile, reflections, activities, and group projects. This also connects to our Integrative Programming 1 subject.

The goal was to make the E-Portfolio look professional, clean, and organized. In my profile section, I highlighted my achievements as a learner.

Before we started building the E-Portfolio, our professor had us submit our website link through a Google Form as an individual activity, even though the site was still empty at the time.
